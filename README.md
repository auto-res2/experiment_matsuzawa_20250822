# CAMLTO-Delta: Risk-Aware Carbon-Optimal Closed-Loop Orchestration for Large Language Model Training
> ⚠️ **NOTE:** This research is an automatic research using AIRAS.
## Abstract
Training-time emissions for large language models are rising faster than power grids are decarbonizing, while hourly marginal carbon intensity can vary three to tenfold across regions and within a day. Organizations increasingly operate under carbon budgets and 24/7 clean energy commitments, yet prevailing tools emphasize accounting rather than control, and schedulers typically optimize latency or energy rather than learning progress per kilogram of CO2 under uncertainty. We present CAMLTO-Delta, a receding-horizon controller that decides each hour where, what, and how to train in order to maximize a risk-adjusted Carbon–Progress Index: expected progress divided by the conditional value-at-risk of emissions. The system closes the loop between carbon forecasts and the training stack; coordinates multilevel scaling paths with low-carbon fillers; shapes compute via power caps, parallelism, and MoE gating; attributes operational, embodied, and network emissions per action; and enforces multi-tenant carbon fairness and optional 24/7 clean energy alignment using dual-based budgets with regret guarantees. We implement CAMLTO-Delta in a PyTorch-first stack and evaluate three scenarios: single-tenant closed-loop control, carbon-aware multilevel orchestration, and multi-tenant scheduling with budgets and 24/7 alignment. The suite yields auditable per-action carbon breakdowns, CPI trajectories, and fairness and alignment reports, enabling rigorous comparisons to energy-only and placement baselines without assuming oracle forecasts [faiz_2023_llmcarbon, banerjee_2019_inductive, bamas_2020_learning, balkanski_2024_energy, zhao_2024_nas, zou_2024_multi, artetxe_2021_efficient, chen_2023_pipeline].
