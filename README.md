# Strongly Adaptive Multi-Resolution Conformal Control for Near Time-Uniform Coverage
> ⚠️ **NOTE:** This research is an automatic research using AIRAS.
## Abstract
We study online uncertainty control for a single streaming time series in settings where operational actions depend on short-horizon reliability and on limiting streaks of misses. Existing online conformal controllers such as ACI and conformal PID stabilize long-run average miscoverage but can under-cover for long stretches after regime shifts; cross-sectional methods like TQA presuppose exchangeability across series and can yield effectively unbounded intervals; offline multi-horizon methods such as CopulaCPTS do not operate in streaming and rely on cross-sectional structure [angelopoulos_2023_conformal, lin_2022_conformal, sun_2022_copula]. We propose SAMC2, a model-agnostic controller that couples multi-resolution per-scale specialists, a strongly adaptive sleeping-experts aggregator balancing coverage and efficiency, and a dyadic primal–dual feasibility layer powered by anytime-valid confidence sequences. SAMC2 targets any interval and aims to bound the deviation of average error from the nominal level α by a term that scales as the square root of (log K plus log T) over the interval length, while preventing interval blow-up via robust barriers and saturation. We present implementation-ready evaluations on synthetic drift and heavy tails, real electricity load, and an ablation “regime carousel,” comparing against PID/ACI, rolling split conformal, and CUSUM-reset ACI.
