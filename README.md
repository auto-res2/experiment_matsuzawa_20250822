# Context-Conditional Adaptive Distillation: Equalizing Teacher Margin Distributions for Robust and Stable Knowledge Transfer
> ⚠️ **NOTE:** This research is an automatic research using AIRAS.
## Abstract
We study a pervasive but under-addressed failure mode in knowledge distillation (KD): the teacher’s decision margin varies systematically with input context, defined by augmentation states (brightness, contrast, scale, translation, blur) and latent style, which induces context-conditional distribution mismatch. Students inherit this skew, degrading cross-architecture transfer, performance on long-tail and rare-context cells, calibration, and worst-group robustness. Student-driven reweighting can be unstable early [niu_2022_respecting, zhou_2021_rethinking], global temperature schedules ignore contextual sharpness [zheng_2024_knowledge], and ECDF-based shift correction has not been used to regularize teacher context-by-margin distributions in KD [mazaheri_2020_robust]. We introduce Context-Conditional Adaptive Distillation (CCAD), a teacher-statistics–only, plug-in mechanism that discovers contexts without labels, builds per-context empirical CDFs (ECDFs) of a boundary-distance proxy, and uses quantiles to drive three coupled controls: rank-stable intra-context weights, inter-context distributionally robust (DRO) group weights, and a context-adaptive teacher temperature. An optional per-context affine logit transform aligns margin distributions to a global reference. CCAD integrates into standard KD with a two-stage schedule for early stability and zero test-time overhead. We provide complete PyTorch utilities and a rigorous evaluation plan on CIFAR-100 and ImageNet-100 with robustness suites, ablations, and fairness safeguards. Our shared execution artifact validates feasibility and instrumentation but contains no numerical results; we therefore pre-register hypotheses and analysis protocols to enable conclusive verification.
