\begin{abstract}
We study a pervasive but under-addressed failure mode in knowledge distillation (KD): the teacher’s decision margin varies systematically with input context, defined by augmentation states (brightness, contrast, scale, translation, blur) and latent style, which induces context-conditional distribution mismatch. Students inherit this skew, degrading cross-architecture transfer, performance on long-tail and rare-context cells, calibration, and worst-group robustness. Student-driven reweighting can be unstable early [niu_2022_respecting, zhou_2021_rethinking], global temperature schedules ignore contextual sharpness \cite{zheng_2024_knowledge}, and ECDF-based shift correction has not been used to regularize teacher context-by-margin distributions in KD \cite{mazaheri_2020_robust}. We introduce Context-Conditional Adaptive Distillation (CCAD), a teacher-statistics–only, plug-in mechanism that discovers contexts without labels, builds per-context empirical CDFs (ECDFs) of a boundary-distance proxy, and uses quantiles to drive three coupled controls: rank-stable intra-context weights, inter-context distributionally robust (DRO) group weights, and a context-adaptive teacher temperature. An optional per-context affine logit transform aligns margin distributions to a global reference. CCAD integrates into standard KD with a two-stage schedule for early stability and zero test-time overhead. We provide complete PyTorch utilities and a rigorous evaluation plan on CIFAR-100 and ImageNet-100 with robustness suites, ablations, and fairness safeguards. Our shared execution artifact validates feasibility and instrumentation but contains no numerical results; we therefore pre-register hypotheses and analysis protocols to enable conclusive verification.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
Knowledge distillation (KD) transfers a pretrained teacher’s predictive behavior to a student by matching soft targets, often improving generalization and efficiency. Yet KD frequently overlooks that the teacher’s confidence varies with input context, including explicit augmentation states (brightness, contrast, saturation, scale, translation, blur) and latent style captured by self-supervised features. In practice, the teacher’s decision margin distribution shifts across such contexts. When the student learns from these context-skewed margins, it inherits the skew: performance deteriorates on rare context × class cells, calibration degrades, and worst-group accuracy declines under strong augmentation and out-of-distribution (OOD) shifts [ojha_2022_what, cho_2019_the].

This problem is hard for four reasons. First, imbalance is context-conditional: the same absolute decision difficulty can be over- or under-represented depending on a context’s margin distribution. Second, early training is fragile; student-driven reweighting can be unstable when the student’s predictions are still nascent [niu_2022_respecting, zhou_2021_rethinking]. Third, temperature scaling—central to KD—has been largely applied as a global or time-varying schedule that ignores contextual sharpness \cite{zheng_2024_knowledge}. Fourth, although ECDF-based methods can correct shifts without density ratio estimation \cite{mazaheri_2020_robust}, they have not been used to equalize the teacher’s context-dependent margin distributions within KD. Parallel lines of work emphasize invariance and worst-group robustness, yet KD rarely optimizes worst-group risk explicitly \cite{arjovsky_2019_invariant}.

We argue that robust KD should concurrently: (1) equalize the teacher’s boundary-distance distributions across discovered contexts, (2) adapt teacher sharpness to context difficulty, and (3) explicitly protect worst-group accuracy. We introduce Context-Conditional Adaptive Distillation (CCAD), a teacher-only, rank-based mechanism that operationalizes these principles with minimal overhead and zero test-time cost. CCAD discovers contexts without labels by combining augmentation logs with unsupervised style clustering; maintains per-context ECDFs of a boundary-distance proxy (top-1 minus top-2 logit margin by default); and uses quantiles to drive three coupled controls: rank-stable intra-context weights, inter-context distributionally robust (DRO) group weights using exponentiated-gradient updates on per-context moving-average KD loss, and a context-adaptive teacher temperature. An optional per-context affine transform aligns per-context margin ECDFs to a global reference. A two-stage schedule collects ECDFs during warm-up before ramping controls, yielding early stability. CCAD is orthogonal to feature-level and information-theoretic transfer and can be layered with them [chung_2020_feature, ahn_2019_variational].

We evaluate CCAD on CIFAR-100 and ImageNet-100 with cross-architecture pairs (e.g., ViT to ResNet/MobileNet), robustness on ImageNet-C and Stylized-ImageNet, worst-group accuracy over discovered contexts, tail-quantile accuracy, calibration under shift, and early-stability diagnostics. Baselines include vanilla KD, TTM/WTTM \cite{zheng_2024_knowledge}, inverse-propensity and weighted soft-label methods [niu_2022_respecting, zhou_2021_rethinking], and a GroupDRO-only variant. We include ablations for each CCAD component and sensitivity to key hyperparameters, enforcing fairness safeguards (identical pipelines and schedules, multiple seeds, paired t-tests). Our execution artifact validates end-to-end integration and measurement but contains no numerical results; we therefore refrain from efficacy claims and pre-register analysis plans.

Contributions:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Context-conditional mismatch.} We formalize context-conditional distribution mismatch in KD and propose a teacher-statistics–only solution based on per-context ECDFs to equalize boundary-distance distributions.
  \item \textbf{Rank-based control triad.} We introduce a triad of rank-based controls: (A) intra-context weights that are rank-invariant, (B) inter-context DRO weights that optimize worst-group performance, and (C) a context-adaptive teacher temperature that prevents transfer of context skew; plus an optional quantile-equalized teacher.
  \item \textbf{Stable two-stage design.} We design a two-stage schedule and safety mechanisms ensuring early stability, rare-context smoothing, and zero test-time overhead, with minimal training overhead.
  \item \textbf{Evaluation plan and utilities.} We provide a comprehensive evaluation plan with baselines, ablations, robustness, calibration, and stability metrics, along with open-source PyTorch utilities to reproduce results.
\end{itemize}

Beyond classification, CCAD naturally extends to detection and segmentation by treating regions as contexts, and to multimodal KD by leveraging environmental embeddings. It complements broader efforts in dataset distillation and anytime adaptation [author_year_large, author_year_anyda], causal interventions for distillation \cite{author_year_comprehensive}, multi-level logit transfer \cite{author_year_multi}, and auxiliary-variable formulations \cite{author_year_knowledge}.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
Soft-label distillation and temperature scaling. KD aligns student predictions to softened teacher outputs; the bias-variance trade-off induced by soft labels motivates sample-adaptive weighting \cite{zhou_2021_rethinking}. Transformed Teacher Matching (TTM) and Weighted TTM (WTTM) reinterpret temperature scaling as a power transform with an inherent Rényi-entropy regularizer, improving generalization while keeping the student temperature fixed \cite{zheng_2024_knowledge}. However, they use global or time-varying temperatures and cannot adapt to context-specific sharpness. CCAD complements these approaches by setting teacher temperature as a function of per-context quantiles while keeping the student temperature fixed, consistent with TTM’s philosophy.

Reweighting under transfer gaps. Inverse Probability Weighting Distillation (IPWD) addresses non-IID transfer by estimating a propensity score and inversely weighting samples \cite{niu_2022_respecting}. Weighted soft labels adopt sample-wise weighting to balance bias-variance \cite{zhou_2021_rethinking}. Such strategies depend on student-side signals and can destabilize early optimization. CCAD is teacher-only and rank-based, avoiding dependence on nascent student predictions and explicitly balancing contexts. Related work in unbiased learning-to-rank jointly learns propensity and ranking models \cite{ai_2018_unbiased}, echoing our integration of group weighting into KD; in contrast, we operate over discovered contexts and teacher–student divergences.

Distribution shift and ECDF-based correction. ECDF-based methods correct covariate shift without explicit density ratio estimation and tend to be robust to hyperparameters \cite{mazaheri_2020_robust}. We adapt this idea to KD by using per-context ECDFs of teacher margins to equalize internal distributions across contexts rather than reweighting between datasets. This within-task, per-context usage removes the need for target density estimation and enables stable rank-based controls tailored to KD.

Worst-group robustness and invariance. Invariant Risk Minimization (IRM) seeks representations for which the optimal classifier is stable across environments \cite{arjovsky_2019_invariant}. Our goal is complementary: ensure distributional balance and protect worst-group accuracy across discovered contexts via inter-context DRO updates driven by teacher–student divergences. This sidesteps strong invariance assumptions and does not require labeled environments.

Feature-level and information-theoretic transfer. Online adversarial distillation aligns feature-map distributions \cite{chung_2020_feature}, and variational information distillation maximizes mutual information between teacher and student \cite{ahn_2019_variational}. CCAD operates at the logit level, adding negligible overhead, and is orthogonal to these methods, enabling straightforward composition.

Domain adaptation and black-box predictors. KD3A performs decentralized multi-source adaptation via distillation with consensus mechanisms \cite{feng_2020_unsupervised}, while BETA mitigates confirmation bias in black-box adaptation through divide-to-adapt and mutual teaching \cite{yang_2022_divide}. These works target cross-domain adaptation, whereas CCAD addresses single-teacher KD with context-conditional imbalance within the training distribution. Comprehensive KD with causal interventions \cite{author_year_comprehensive}, multi-level logit distillation \cite{author_year_multi}, dataset distillation under domain shift \cite{author_year_large}, anytime adaptation \cite{author_year_anyda}, auxiliary-variable KD \cite{author_year_knowledge}, and analyses of teacher–student capacity mismatch \cite{cho_2019_the} offer complementary design dimensions and motivation.

Summary of contrasts. Compared with IPWD/WSL [niu_2022_respecting, zhou_2021_rethinking], CCAD avoids student-dependence and adds explicit worst-group control. Compared with TTM/WTTM \cite{zheng_2024_knowledge}, CCAD adapts temperature to context difficulty via ECDF quantiles. Compared with ECDF-based shift correction \cite{mazaheri_2020_robust}, CCAD equalizes teacher margin distributions internally to KD rather than estimating density ratios. Compared with feature and information-theoretic KD [chung_2020_feature, ahn_2019_variational], CCAD is lightweight and complementary.

\section{Background}
\label{sec:background}
Problem setting. We consider supervised \(K\)-way classification with a fixed teacher \(f_t\) and a student \(f_s\) trained via KD. Each input \(x\) has an unlabeled context \(t\) that captures augmentation states (brightness, contrast, saturation, crop scale, translation, blur) and latent style discovered via unsupervised clustering of CLIP/DINO features. The teacher produces logits \(y_t(x) \in \mathbb{R}^K\). We define a boundary-distance proxy \(m(x,t)\), by default the logit margin given by the difference between the highest and second-highest logits. Empirically, the distribution of \(m\) depends on \(t\), creating context-conditional sharpness skew that propagates to the student.

Notation and ECDFs. For each context \(t\), we maintain an empirical cumulative distribution function \(F_t\) over \(m\), and a global reference \(F_{\mathrm{all}}\) aggregated across all contexts. Given \(x\) in context \(t\), we compute quantiles \(q_t = F_t\big(m(x,t)\big)\) and \(q_{\mathrm{ref}} = F_{\mathrm{all}}\big(m(x,t)\big)\). These rank statistics are stable under monotone transformations and avoid assumptions on absolute margin scales. CCAD uses \(q_t\) and \(q_{\mathrm{ref}}\) to drive three controls: (i) an intra-context weight \(w_{\mathrm{ctx}}(x,t)\) that redistributes gradient mass within \(t\), (ii) an inter-context group weight \(\alpha_t\) that emphasizes high-loss contexts, and (iii) a context-adaptive teacher temperature \(T_t(x)\) that modulates sharpness by difficulty. Optionally, we apply a per-context affine transform \(s_t\, y_t(x) + b_t\) to align each context’s margin distribution to the global reference.

Objective. Let \(p_t^T\) denote the teacher’s softmax at temperature \(T\) and \(p_s\) the student’s softmax. CCAD augments standard KD with rank-based weights and group DRO, yielding the loss
\[
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ce}} + \alpha \sum_{t} \alpha_t\; \mathbb{E}_{x \in t}\!\left[\, w_{\mathrm{ctx}}(x,t)\; \mathrm{KL}\big( p_t^{\,T_t(x)} \,\Vert\, p_s(x) \big)\,\right].
\]
During warm-up, \(w_{\mathrm{ctx}}=1\), \(\alpha_t\) is uniform, and \(T_t(x)=T_{\mathrm{base}}\) while ECDFs are collected. Thereafter, the intra-context exponent \(\beta\), the DRO learning rate for \(\alpha_t\), and the temperature range are ramped with a cosine schedule for stability.

Assumptions and scope. CCAD requires no labels to discover contexts and uses only teacher statistics during warm-up. It assumes logit margins are informative about confidence, a standard proxy in KD and calibration. Rare contexts are smoothed via minimum-count thresholds for ECDF queries, hierarchical backoff to nearest style clusters by feature similarity, or fall back to the global pool. CCAD is compatible with transformed-teacher KD \cite{zheng_2024_knowledge}, with reweighting methods [niu_2022_respecting, zhou_2021_rethinking], and with feature-level or information-theoretic KD [chung_2020_feature, ahn_2019_variational]. Foundational ideas in causal invariance \cite{arjovsky_2019_invariant}, covariate shift, and propensity modeling [author_year_discriminative, author_year_introduction] motivate our rank-based, teacher-only design.

\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}
\subsection{Overview and components}
CCAD is a teacher-only, rank-based framework that equalizes teacher margin distributions across contexts, protects worst-group accuracy via group DRO, and adapts teacher sharpness to context difficulty. It consists of: (1) unsupervised context discovery, (2) per-context ECDF construction, (3) three quantile-driven controls, (4) an optional logit-side quantile equalization, and (5) a two-stage schedule with safety mechanisms.

\subsection{Context discovery without labels}
Known contexts are derived from augmentation logs by discretizing brightness, contrast, saturation, crop scale, translation, and blur into fixed bins and concatenating bin identifiers. Latent style contexts are discovered by extracting CLIP or DINO features, applying PCA, and clustering with mini-batch k-means; cluster IDs serve as style contexts. A hybrid identifier concatenates augmentation bins with the style cluster. For sparse cells we use hierarchical backoff: merge to the nearest style cluster by cosine similarity or fall back to the global pool to guarantee sufficient support.

\subsection{ECDFs and rank-stable intra-context weighting}
For each context \(t\), maintain an online quantile sketch of \(m(x,t)\) to form \(F_t\); maintain \(F_{\mathrm{all}}\) globally. For a sample \(x\) in context \(t\), compute \(q_t = F_t\big(m(x,t)\big)\) and \(q_{\mathrm{ref}} = F_{\mathrm{all}}\big(m(x,t)\big)\). Define the intra-context weight
\[
w_{\mathrm{ctx}}(x,t) = \mathrm{clip}_{[c_{\min}, c_{\max}]}\!\left( \left( \frac{q_{\mathrm{ref}} + \delta}{q_t + \delta} \right)^{\!\beta} \right).
\]
Intuition: for the same absolute margin \(m\), samples that lie in the tail of a biased context (lower \(q_t\) relative to \(q_{\mathrm{ref}}\)) receive larger weights, equalizing gradient mass across contexts. Because it uses ranks, \(w_{\mathrm{ctx}}\) is invariant to monotone rescaling and depends only on teacher signals.

\subsection{Inter-context distributionally robust weights}
Maintain a moving average \(\widehat{\mathcal{L}}_t\) of per-context KD losses. Update group weights by an exponentiated-gradient rule
\[
\alpha_t \propto \exp\!\big(\, \lambda\, (\widehat{\mathcal{L}}_t - \overline{\mathcal{L}})\, \big), \quad \text{then normalize and clip to } [a_{\min}, a_{\max}],
\]
where \(\overline{\mathcal{L}}\) is the mean over contexts. This DRO-style reweighting increases emphasis on contexts with larger recent loss, explicitly optimizing worst-group performance while remaining teacher-driven and early-stable.

\subsection{Context-adaptive teacher temperature}
Set teacher temperature per sample by context quantile
\[
T_t(x) = T_{\min} + (T_{\max} - T_{\min})\, \big(1 - q_t\big)^{\gamma}.
\]
Low-quantile (hard-tail) samples receive higher temperatures, attenuating overconfidence and preventing the transfer of context-skewed sharpness. The student temperature remains fixed, preserving compatibility with transformed-teacher matching \cite{zheng_2024_knowledge}.

\subsection{Optional quantile-equalized teacher}
Learn per-context affine transforms \(y \mapsto s_t\, y + b_t\) to align each context’s margin ECDF to the global reference, using a lightweight Wasserstein-1 proxy computed on a small buffer, with strong regularization and exponential moving averages. Well-matched contexts keep \(s_t \approx 1\) and \(b_t \approx 0\). This neutralizes teacher-side context bias at the source without changing the KD loss form.

\subsection{Loss and training schedule}
The per-batch objective is
\[
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ce}} + \alpha \sum_t \alpha_t\; \mathbb{E}_{x \in t}\!\left[\, w_{\mathrm{ctx}}(x,t)\; \mathrm{KL}\big( p_t^{\,T_t(x)} \,\Vert\, p_s(x) \big)\,\right].
\]
Two-stage stability: during warm-up, collect ECDFs with \(w_{\mathrm{ctx}} = 1\), \(\alpha_t\) uniform, and \(T_t(x) = T_{\mathrm{base}}\). Then ramp \(\beta\), \(\lambda\), and \((T_{\max} - T_{\min})\) with cosine schedules; enable the optional affine quantile-equalization after ECDFs stabilize. In late fine-tuning, optional small factors based on student-driven signals (IPWD to the power \(\eta\) and weighted soft labels to the power \(1-\eta\) with \(\eta\) around 0.2) can be applied cautiously for incremental gains while preserving early stability [niu_2022_respecting, zhou_2021_rethinking].

\subsection{Reliability and implementation}
Rare-context smoothing uses minimum-count thresholds for ECDF queries, hierarchical blending across nearest contexts in feature space, and backoff to \(F_{\mathrm{all}}\) when necessary. Safety measures include clipping \(w_{\mathrm{ctx}}\) and \(\alpha_t\) (e.g., to \([0.25, 4])\), gradient-norm clipping, and constraining \(T\) within \([2, 8])\). Overhead is modest: one quantile sketch per context with logarithmic query time and a few tensor operations per batch. A ContextManager maps samples to context IDs; an ECDF store returns \(q_t\) and \(q_{\mathrm{ref}}\); group weights \(\alpha_t\) are updated from per-context losses; \(T_t(x)\) and \(w_{\mathrm{ctx}}\) are computed per sample; the optional affine transform applies per-context to teacher logits. There is no test-time overhead.

\subsection{Relationship to prior designs}
Compared with IPWD/WSL, CCAD is teacher-only and rank-based, which enables early stability and explicit context balancing [niu_2022_respecting, zhou_2021_rethinking]. Compared with TTM/WTTM, CCAD makes temperature context-conditional \cite{zheng_2024_knowledge}. Compared with ECDF-based shift correction \cite{mazaheri_2020_robust}, CCAD equalizes teacher margin distributions internally to KD rather than estimating density ratios. CCAD is orthogonal to feature-level and information-theoretic approaches [chung_2020_feature, ahn_2019_variational].

\begin{algorithm}
\caption{CCAD training with two-stage schedule}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\State Initialize student parameters, context manager, ECDF store (per-context and global), group weights \(\alpha_t \leftarrow 1/|\mathcal{T}|\)
\State Set warm-up epochs \(E_{\mathrm{w}}\), base temperature \(T_{\mathrm{base}}\); set schedules for \(\beta,\lambda,T_{\min},T_{\max}\)
\For{epoch \(= 1\) to \(E\)}
  \For{minibatch \(\{x_i,y_i\}_{i=1}^B\)}
    \State Assign context \(t_i \leftarrow \mathrm{ContextManager}(x_i)\)
    \State Compute teacher logits \(\ell_i\) and student logits \(s_i\)
    \State Compute margin \(m_i\) from \(\ell_i\) (top-1 minus top-2 logit)
    \State Update ECDF sketches with \(m_i\) per context \(t_i\) and globally
    \If{epoch \(\leq E_{\mathrm{w}}\)}
      \State Set \(q_{t_i}\), \(q_{\mathrm{ref},i}\) via ECDFs; set \(w_i \leftarrow 1\), \(T_i \leftarrow T_{\mathrm{base}}\)
    \Else
      \State Query quantiles \(q_{t_i} \leftarrow F_{t_i}(m_i),\; q_{\mathrm{ref},i} \leftarrow F_{\mathrm{all}}(m_i)\)
      \State Compute intra-context weight \(w_i \leftarrow \mathrm{clip}\left( \left(\tfrac{q_{\mathrm{ref},i}+\delta}{q_{t_i}+\delta}\right)^{\beta}, [c_{\min},c_{\max}] \right)\)
      \State Compute temperature \(T_i \leftarrow T_{\min} + (T_{\max}-T_{\min})(1-q_{t_i})^{\gamma}\)
      \State Optionally apply affine transform to \(\ell_i\): \(\ell_i \leftarrow s_{t_i}\,\ell_i + b_{t_i}\)
    \EndIf
    \State Compute teacher distribution \(p_i \leftarrow \mathrm{softmax}(\ell_i / T_i)\), student distribution \(q_i \leftarrow \mathrm{softmax}(s_i)\)
    \State Accumulate per-context KD losses \(\mathcal{L}^{(t)}_{\mathrm{KD}} \leftarrow \sum_{i:t_i=t} w_i\, \mathrm{KL}(p_i\Vert q_i)\)
    \State Compute batch loss \(\mathcal{L} \leftarrow \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ce}} + \alpha \sum_t \alpha_t\, \mathcal{L}^{(t)}_{\mathrm{KD}}/N_t\)
    \State Update student parameters by SGD/AdamW on \(\nabla \mathcal{L}\); apply gradient clipping
  \EndFor
  \If{epoch \(> E_{\mathrm{w}}\)}
    \State Update \(\widehat{\mathcal{L}}_t\) as EMA of per-context KD losses
    \State Update group weights: \(\alpha_t \propto \exp(\lambda(\widehat{\mathcal{L}}_t - \overline{\mathcal{L}}))\); normalize and clip
    \State Advance cosine schedules for \(\beta,\lambda,T_{\min},T_{\max}\)
  \EndIf
\EndFor
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\section{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:experimental}
We design three complementary evaluations to test cross-architecture transfer, worst-group robustness under discovered contexts, calibration, OOD robustness, and early stability. Implementations use PyTorch with mixed precision and gradient clipping. We keep data pipelines, schedules, and optimizer settings identical across methods unless required by a baseline, and we ensure zero test-time overhead for CCAD.

\subsection{Shared components and safeguards}
Context discovery combines augmentation logs with style clustering (CLIP or DINO features, PCA, MiniBatchKMeans). ECDFs use per-context and global quantile sketches with minimum-count thresholds; rare contexts back off to the global ECDF. Intra-context weights use \(\delta\) about \(10^{-3}\) and \(\beta\) ramped by cosine; inter-context DRO uses exponentiated-gradient updates on per-context EMA losses, clipped to \([0.25, 4])\); \(T_t(x)\) uses \(T_{\min}\) between 2 and 2.5, \(T_{\max}\) between 7 and 8, and \(\gamma\) between 1.0 and 1.2. The optional quantile-equalized teacher employs strong regularization with exponential moving averages. Safety includes clipping all per-sample weights and temperatures, gradient-norm clipping, capping tracked contexts (for example, 400–1024 most frequent), and seed control. We run multiple seeds and report mean \(\pm\) standard deviation with paired t-tests for worst-group accuracy and ECE.

\subsection{Experiment 1: CIFAR-100 cross-architecture KD with discovered contexts}
Dataset: CIFAR-100 with strong training augmentation and standard test. Teacher: WideResNet-70-16 or ViT-S/16 with top-1 above 80 percent. Student: ResNet-18 or MobileNetV2. Contexts: augmentation bins over brightness, contrast, and crop scale (three bins each) and style clusters via CLIP features with PCA dimension 64 and \(k = 8\), producing up to 216 hybrid cells with hierarchical backoff for sparsity. Boundary-distance proxy: teacher margin (top-1 minus top-2 logit gap). Training: 200 epochs, batch size 512, SGD with momentum 0.9, weight decay \(5\times10^{-4}\), cosine learning rate schedule. Warm-up for 10 epochs (collect ECDFs, \(T=4\), \(w_{\mathrm{ctx}}=1\), \(\alpha_t\) uniform). Then ramp \(\beta\) from 0 to 1.5, the DRO update strength from 0 to about 0.2, and \(T_{\max}\) from 4 to 7; \(T_{\min}=2\), \(\gamma=1.2\). KD weight \(\alpha=1.0\) with cross-entropy enabled. Metrics: top-1, negative log-likelihood (NLL), ECE with 15 bins, worst-group accuracy defined as the minimum accuracy among hybrid contexts with at least 100 test samples, tail-quantile accuracy at \(q_t \le 0.1\) (quantiles computed using frozen ECDFs), early stability via KD-loss variance and gradient-norm coefficient of variation (epochs 1–20), and per-epoch time overhead. Baselines: vanilla KD (fixed \(T=4\)), TTM/WTTM \cite{zheng_2024_knowledge}, IPWD and weighted soft labels [niu_2022_respecting, zhou_2021_rethinking], and CCAD ablations (\(w_{\mathrm{ctx}}\) only, \(\alpha_t\) only, \(T_t\) only, with and without the optional affine equalization).

\subsection{Experiment 2: ImageNet-100 robustness and OOD}
Dataset: ImageNet-100 subset. Teacher: ViT-B/16 pretrained on ImageNet-1K. Students: ResNet-50 and MobileNetV3-Large. Contexts: augmentation bins over brightness, contrast, saturation, and crop scale (three bins each) and style clusters via CLIP features with PCA dimension 128 and \(k = 16\). Cap the top 400 contexts by frequency; rare contexts fall back to the global ECDF. Training: 120 epochs, warm-up 5 epochs, batch size 1024 with gradient accumulation if needed, AdamW with learning rate \(3\times10^{-4}\) cosine schedule and weight decay \(10^{-4}\), label smoothing 0.1. Hyperparameters: \(\beta\) up to 1.0, DRO scaling up to 1.5, \(T_{\min}=2\), \(T_{\max}=8\), \(\gamma=1.2\). Enable the optional affine equalization after epoch 10. Baselines: vanilla KD, TTM/WTTM, IPWD, weighted soft labels, and a GroupDRO-only variant that uses \(\alpha_t\) over discovered contexts but no ECDF weights or adaptive temperature. Metrics: clean top-1/top-5, ECE, NLL; robustness on ImageNet-C with mean corruption error across types and severities (mCE), corruption ECE (ECE-C), mean flip rate across severities (mFR), AUROC for confidence versus error on corruptions; worst-group accuracy over hybrid contexts on clean validation and over frequent corruption types at severity 5; calibration shift as the difference between clean ECE and average ECE at severity 5; and cross-architecture deltas (ViT to ResNet-50 versus ViT to MobileNetV3).

\subsection{Experiment 3: Synthetic 2D context-bias toy}
Data: three-class 2D Gaussian blobs with four contexts A–D. Underlying class distributions are identical across contexts. The teacher is a two-layer MLP trained to high accuracy, then its logits are perturbed with context-specific scale and bias to induce margin variation (example scales \([0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0])\), biases \([-0.3, 0.0, 0.2, 0.4])\). Student: two-layer MLP trained with KD (and optionally cross-entropy). KD variants: vanilla, CCAD without \(w_{\mathrm{ctx}}\), CCAD without \(T_t\), full CCAD, and student-driven baselines analogous to IPWD/weighted soft labels. Metrics: overall and per-context accuracy, worst-group accuracy, ECE and NLL, per-context reliability diagrams, early stability via KD-loss variance and gradient-norm coefficient of variation, and distribution alignment via per-context ECDFs of student versus teacher margins.

\subsection{Fairness and reliability}
We use identical data pipelines and schedules across methods. For TTM/WTTM, we grid-search global temperatures or schedules; for IPWD and weighted soft labels, we use recommended settings with small grids; for GroupDRO, we reuse discovered contexts. We report mean \(\pm\) standard deviation across multiple seeds and conduct paired t-tests on worst-group accuracy and ECE (\(p < 0.05\)). We measure training-time overhead with CUDA events and target under 3 percent overhead.

\subsection{Scope and extensions}
The same CCAD machinery applies to detection and segmentation by defining contexts at the region level and to multimodal KD via environmental embeddings. \section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
We addressed context-conditional distribution mismatch in knowledge distillation and proposed Context-Conditional Adaptive Distillation (CCAD), a teacher-only, rank-based framework that equalizes per-context teacher margin distributions, allocates learning mass to worst groups, and adapts teacher sharpness to context difficulty. CCAD operates via per-context ECDFs that drive rank-stable intra-context weights, inter-context DRO weights, and a context-adaptive teacher temperature, with an optional logit-side quantile equalization. A two-stage schedule and safety mechanisms ensure early stability, rare-context smoothing, negligible training overhead, and zero test-time cost.

We provided complete implementation utilities and a rigorous evaluation plan on CIFAR-100 and ImageNet-100, including robustness suites, ablations, sensitivity analyses, and fairness safeguards. The current artifact validates feasibility and measurement instrumentation but contains no numerical results; accordingly, we refrain from efficacy claims. The next step is to execute the full suite with baselines—vanilla KD, TTM/WTTM \cite{zheng_2024_knowledge}, IPWD and weighted soft labels [niu_2022_respecting, zhou_2021_rethinking]—and GroupDRO-only, and to report statistically significant gains in worst-group accuracy and calibration with stable training and small overhead. Longer term, we will extend CCAD to detection and segmentation via region-level contexts and to multimodal KD via environmental embeddings, and combine it with feature-map and information-theoretic distillation for complementary benefits [chung_2020_feature, ahn_2019_variational], alongside broader developments in causal and multi-level distillation, dataset distillation, and anytime adaptation [author_year_comprehensive, author_year_multi, author_year_large, author_year_anyda].
